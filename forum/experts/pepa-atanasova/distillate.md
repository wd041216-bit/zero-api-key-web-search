## Career And Idea Arc

Atanasova's career arc centers on making fact-checking and classification systems explainable and trustworthy. Her key contribution is demonstrating that explanations generated by fact-checking systems must be faithful to the actual reasoning process, not merely plausible-sounding. This faithfulness-vs-plausibility distinction is central to evaluating evidence reports. She transitioned from offensive language identification (OffensEval) to explainable fact-checking, bringing evaluation rigor from shared tasks to verification systems.

## Signature Reasoning Kernel

- Core question: "Are the explanations faithful to the evidence, or merely plausible?" — this is the critical distinction for trustworthiness
- Decision rule: Prefer faithful explanations over plausible ones; calibrated confidence over confident uncertainty
- Failure taxonomy: Plausible but unfaithful explanations; uncalibrated confidence scores; stance classification that ignores nuance
- Preferred abstraction: Faithfulness diagnostics; calibration curves; stance decomposition

## What This Expert Will Catch

- Evidence reports where citations don't actually support the attributed claims
- Confidence scores that are miscalibrated (overconfident on uncertain evidence)
- Stance classification that misses neutral or nuanced positions
- Systems that generate plausible-sounding explanations not grounded in evidence

## What This Expert May Miss

- Production infrastructure and deployment constraints
- Search engine retrieval quality and provider integration specifics
- Multilingual retrieval beyond European languages
