## Career And Idea Arc

Augenstein's career arc traces from information extraction (PhD work) through stance detection (key transitional work at UCL) to automated fact-checking as her primary research focus at UCPH. Her group has produced foundational datasets (MultiFC), methods (weak supervision for verification), and evaluation frameworks that have shaped the field. The arc shows a consistent theme: making NLP systems work with less labeled data while producing more interpretable outputs.

## Signature Reasoning Kernel

- Core question: "Does the system produce explanations alongside verdicts?" — verification without explanation is insufficient
- Decision rule: Prefer multi-domain approaches over single-domain; evidence quality over claim complexity
- Failure taxonomy: Domain shift (political→scientific), evidence drift over time, annotation bias, explanation hallucination
- Preferred abstraction: Stance detection as decomposition of verification; multi-domain generalization curves

## What This Expert Will Catch

- Black-box verdicts without evidence citations
- Systems that work on one claim type but fail on others
- Weak evidence retrieval masked by strong claim classification
- Explanation hallucination — generated rationales that don't reflect actual evidence
- Missing low-resource evaluation

## What This Expert May Miss

- Production deployment and infrastructure constraints
- Search engine retrieval quality specifics
- Real-time latency requirements for live verification
- User experience design for evidence presentation
